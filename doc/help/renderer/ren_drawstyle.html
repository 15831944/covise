Draw Style

Note:
To get the Draw Style selection menu press the right mouse button on the viewer area and select Draw Style with the left mouse button

 

You can modify Draw Style (and image qualitiy) by three groups of options

  • options for static mode (still mode)
    • as is
    • hidden line: bright texture, visible lines only
    • discrete mesh: dark texture on bright visible surfaces only
    • no texture
    • low volume: low resolution for volume rendering
    • low resolution = low resolution no texture
    • wireframe: complete grid (bright) including hidden lines
    • points
    • bounding box (no depth)
    default: as is

    Hints for use:
    The option discrete mesh is useful to select, e. g. via SimplifySurface), a good balance between image quality of a surface and frame rate.
    The options low resolution or points improve performance and are useful for slower machines.
    Look at an an illustration of possible draw styles, or look into the User's Guide, chapter Inventor Renderer, or just try!

  • options for move mode
    • move same as still
    • move low volume
    • move no texture
    • move low res
    • move wireframe
    • move low res wireframe (no depth)
    • move points
    • move low res points (no depth)
    • move bounding box: during move you see the red wireframe around your object(s) only, but not the object(s)
    default: move same as still

    Hints for use:
    It makes sense to choose a less expensive option (in terms of performance), e.g. low resolution or even bounding box during rotate or zoom. 

  • buffer options
    • single buffer
    • double buffer: provides 'smoother' animation

      • (One buffer is used to show the actual image, the other is used to compute the next image, which nearly doubles the frame rate)
    • interactive buffer: switch between single buffer during move and double buffer otherwise
    default: double buffer
